Our Story - Shreya Srivastava

My name is Shreya Srivastava, and I currently work as a software developer. Beyond my professional life, I am a passionate technical enthusiast, but today, I want to share a deeply personal and emotional journey that has shaped who I am. This is a story of a significant challenge that tested my family’s strength and faith.

The story begins on May 21, 2018. It was my parents’ anniversary, and we were celebrating together, filled with happiness and joy. The day was beautiful, but little did we know that our lives were about to change drastically. The very next day, my father fell seriously ill. We rushed him to a hospital in Prayagraj, where the doctors diagnosed him with liver cirrhosis and confirmed that water had accumulated in his liver. His treatment began immediately. For five days, the doctors worked tirelessly, draining the fluid from his liver using injections. However, after those five days, the doctors informed us that his condition was critical and referred us to another hospital for further treatment.

Our next stop was PGI Lucknow, where we received devastating news—my father was diagnosed with liver cancer. The doctors explained that the only way to save him was through a liver transplant. In that moment, our world came crashing down. The thought of losing my father within six months, as predicted by the doctors, was unbearable. As the eldest daughter, I knew I had to stay strong for my family, even though I was only 17 years old at the time. While everyone else was consumed by grief, I held onto my faith and trusted God’s plan.

Determined to fight for my father’s life, I began searching for the best liver surgeon in India. We traveled far and wide, visiting renowned hospitals like Gangaram Hospital, Medanta, Apollo, and centers in Hyderabad, Chennai, and Bangalore. After months of relentless effort and countless hospital visits, we finally arrived at Max Hospital in Saket, Delhi. There, we met Dr. Subash Gupta, the chairman of Liver and Biliary Sciences. He became our beacon of hope, initiating my father’s treatment and confirming that a liver transplant was his only chance of survival.

The next challenge was finding a donor. My mother volunteered to donate her liver, but she failed the rigorous donor evaluation process. Desperate and heartbroken, my mother pleaded with family members to step forward, but no one agreed. It was then that I decided to take a stand. I told my mother, “I will donate my liver.” Initially, my parents were against the idea. My mother argued that I was too young, and my father couldn’t bear the thought of me undergoing such a procedure. But I was determined. After convincing them, I began the donor evaluation process, which involved six stages of comprehensive testing—from liver function tests, CT scans, and MRIs to psychological evaluations and interviews with the Transplant Health Organization (THO).

One significant complication was that my blood group, AB+, did not match my father’s, which was B+. This required an ABO-incompatible liver transplantation (LT). To prepare for this, my father underwent immunosuppressive treatments to lower his antibody levels, and his body was closely monitored. This preparation took four long months. Finally, on the night of September 5, 2018, the parameters came into range. At around 2 a.m., I was informed that the operation theater was being prepped for my father’s transplant.

By 5 a.m., nurses came to prepare me for surgery. I took a bath, and my mother tied my hair into a ponytail. I hugged my parents tightly, and soon, I was wheeled into the operation theater. As the donor, I went in first. The medical team kept me warm and began administering anesthesia. By 6:30 a.m., I was unconscious. My father was brought in shortly after, around 7 a.m. The surgeries were long and intense—mine lasted 16 hours, while my father’s took 20 hours.

When I regained consciousness later that night, I saw Dr. Subash Gupta. My first question was, “How is my father?” With a reassuring smile, he patted my head and said, “The operation was successful.” Those words brought immense relief to my soul, and I drifted back into sleep, still under the effects of anesthesia.

The days that followed were filled with recovery and healing. I stayed in the hospital for 16 days, while my father stayed for 25 days post-operation. Slowly but surely, we regained our strength. When we finally returned to our hometown, Prayagraj, it was with gratitude and renewed hope.

People often say that cancer is unbeatable, but I believe that with faith in God, a skilled doctor like Dr. Subash Gupta, and the unwavering love of family, even the toughest battles can be won. This journey remains close to my heart, as it brings back memories of resilience, sacrifice, and love.

Now, eight years later, my father continues to thrive, and I pray for his health and happiness every day. This experience has inspired me to start AMCARES, dedicated to helping families in similar situations, with the mission of saving as many lives as possible and ensuring that no one has to face such challenges alone.

Thank you for reading my story. Join AMCARES’ mission to make a difference with AI-driven support.

With gratitude,
Shreya Srivastava
